The Hard Numbers

South Africa's vehicle finance market is under real pressure. Here's what the data says.

🚗

Repossessions Are Rising

↑ 21%

Non-performing loans across SA's major banks increased by 21% in 2023, with vehicle and asset finance portfolios driving a significant portion of the R53.8 billion credit loss charge.

Source: PwC Major Banks Analysis 2024
📉

Delinquency Rate

5.14%

Over 109,000 vehicle finance accounts are 3+ months in arrears. That's more than 1 in 20 financed cars at risk of repossession right now.

Source: TransUnion Q3 2024 Industry Insights
💰

Average Loan Size

R400,960

The average new vehicle loan in Q3 2024. With prime at 10.50% and most car loans stretching 60–72 months, the monthly burden is real.

Source: TransUnion Q3 2024 Vehicle Finance Data
🏦

Bank Credit Losses

R53.8B

SA's four major banks (Absa, FirstRand, Nedbank, Standard Bank) charged R53.8 billion for credit losses in 2023 — up 26.6% from the previous year.

Source: PwC Major Banks Analysis 2024
⚖️

Ombudsman Complaints

Rising

The Banking Services Ombudsman has noted a sharp increase in complaints about vehicle repossessions, with many consumers unaware the bank remains the legal owner until the final payment.

Source: OBS South Africa, Oct 2023
📊

Balance at Risk

6.48%

Of the total R521 billion vehicle finance book, 6.48% by balance is in serious delinquency. That's roughly R33.8 billion in loans where the bank is actively considering recovery action.

Source: TransUnion Q3 2024

Why a 3-Month Buffer Matters

Banks can legally repossess a financed vehicle once you default. In South Africa, most lenders issue a Section 129 notice after 20 days of non-payment. By day 90, the repo truck is loading your car.
