🎓
Student · Cape Town
Thandi, 22
Education student, part-time waitress
"I was getting R2,800 a month from waitressing and my NSFAS allowance. By week two I had nothing left — I didn't even know where it went. I'd buy airtime, grab food here and there, and suddenly it was gone. I felt too embarrassed to ask anyone for help because I thought it meant I was bad with money."
🌱 After 3 weeks on MyRandWise, Thandi saw that R680/month was going to small food purchases she didn't even remember making. She set a weekly food budget and saved R400 in her first month — her first savings ever. Now building towards a R2,000 emergency fund.
🔧
Self-employed · Soweto
Sipho, 34
Freelance electrician, 3 kids
"My income is never the same every month. Some months R12,000, some months R4,000. I had a store account, a furniture account, and a personal loan — I was just paying minimums and not getting anywhere. I didn't even know which one to focus on first. Every month felt like I was just surviving."
⚡ MyRandWise showed Sipho his debt snowball — the furniture account would be paid off in 8 months if he put just R200 extra per month on it. He cancelled two subscriptions he'd forgotten about and redirected that money to debt. Debt freedom score improved from 51 to 68 in 6 weeks.
🏠
Domestic worker · Johannesburg
Nomsa, 41
Domestic worker, supports family in Limpopo
"I earn R5,500 a month. I send R1,500 home, I have my transport, food, and a clothing account. I borrowed from a mashonisa once when things were tight — biggest mistake of my life. I needed something simple, not a complicated banking app. Something that speaks to my life."
🤝 MyRandWise showed Nomsa she had R300/month that wasn't allocated to anything — it was just disappearing. She set up a savings goal for her daughter's school uniform. She also used the Mashonisa calculator to see exactly what that loan had really cost her. First time she's ever seen her full financial picture in one place.
💼
Professional · Pretoria
Kagiso, 29
Junior accountant, car loan, renting
"I earn a decent salary but I felt like I had nothing to show for it. I had a car, a good job, but I couldn't save. I kept telling myself I'd start saving 'next month'. I tried Excel spreadsheets but I never kept them updated. I needed something that just told me what I could actually spend this week."
📊 MyRandWise calculated Kagiso's real weekly budget after his car payment, insurance and rent — not just his salary divided by 4. He had R1,150 a week to spend, not the R3,200 he thought. That one number changed everything. Saved R4,800 in 3 months. First savings in 4 years.
